Equal opportunity

Disability Confident scheme

King Recruitment has signed up to the government’s Disability Confident scheme. They want to help disabled people get an interview for this apprenticeship.

This company will try to offer you an interview if you:

  • have a physical impairment, mental impairment or long-term medical condition
  • meet the essential requirements for this apprenticeship
Understanding disability

Almost 1 in 4 working age adults in the UK have a disability. A disability is a long-term physical or mental impairment that has a substantial effect on your daily life.

If you’re unsure whether you should use this scheme, you can check the definition of disability under the Equality Act 2010 (opens in new tab).

The company may not be able to offer everybody who applies under the Disability Confident scheme an interview.If this apprenticeship receives a large amount of applications, the company will still interview a fair number of disabled people who meet the essential requirements.

If you apply under the Disability Confident scheme, you don’t need to provide any information about your condition. If you need support at an interview, you can ask for this in the Request interview adjustments section of your application.
